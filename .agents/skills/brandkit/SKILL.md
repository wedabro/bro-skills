# Brand Kit Image Generation

Create a coherent identity system with a defensible concept, not a collection of unrelated logo mockups.

## Workflow

1. Extract brand audience, category, positioning, personality, competitors, cultural constraints, and required applications.
2. State a short strategic idea and derive two or three distinct logo concepts from meaning, not decoration.
3. Choose one direction and lock logo logic, palette, typography, imagery, layout grid, and material language.
4. Generate a focused board or requested identity assets using realistic, relevant applications.
5. Check small-size recognition, monochrome behavior, consistency, legibility, and category distinctiveness.

## Non-negotiables

- Build the symbol from a clear idea: monogram, product action, metaphor fusion, negative space, or construction geometry.
- Keep logo geometry simple enough to recognize at favicon size.
- Show one coherent system rather than many unrelated styles on one board.
- Use sparse, readable typography and realistic mockups that reveal how the identity behaves.
- Avoid generic orbit marks, random shields, meaningless hexagons, fake embossing, and crowded logo walls.
- Keep palette disciplined and provide monochrome or single-color behavior when relevant.
- Do not fabricate legal claims, awards, partnerships, or cultural symbolism without grounding.
- Treat reference brands as directional evidence, never as shapes to copy.
